Wriggy

Self motivated, analytical thinker with 6 years experience in IT and an MSc in Geophysics

A little history

Following graduation with a degree in physics, I spent a year travelling and working in south east Asia. I returned to further studies with a strong technical component and went on to join the IT Graduate Scheme at North West Water.

The graduate scheme involved placements developing a broad awareness of IT functions. On completion, I worked 5 years as a database administrator. Responsibilities included maintenance, backup and recovery of business critical databases, problem investigation and resolution, and support for development environments with DB2 and Oracle. During this time the team became part of the outsourcing branch of United Utilities and were later transferred to Cap Gemini. I took voluntary redundancy on the birth of my second child.

Since then Iâ€™ve taken on a mixed bag of part-time roles alongside my caring responsibilities - ranging from adult learner supporter and office work to commissioned portrait artist(!). These diverse roles have all taught me something new. But where time allowed or the opportunity arose I returned to tinkering on the computer - creating websites for myself and an art group, learning about digital image manipulation and experimenting with coding puzzles and algorithms.

As caring responsibilities have reduced I have enjoyed more curiosity driven analytical projects bringing my technical skills up to date in the process. I have taken online courses in machine learning and tools for data visualisation. I am fascinated by the visual display of quantitative information. I would love to find a way back to work with a more analytical component, perhaps in data analysis or dashboard development.

Things I can do

I used Python3 in Jupyter Notebooks to analyse local air quality data. This required transforming the data from formatted R objects into Pandas dataframes and dealing with the challenges of missing data, daily and seasonal cycles and 'noise'. I made extensive use of Matplotlib for data visualisations and learned just enough JavaScript and D3 for some interactive visualisations. I have also used Python for various coding puzzles and Matlab (Octave) for some machine learning.

I created a number of simple websites using Wordpress and Wix. Besides using templates I have learned to use cpanel and some HTML and CSS.I can also manipulate digital images using Gimp.

During my time as a database administrator I made extensive use of DB2, Oracle,SQL and pl/SQL. I also, many years ago, learned to program in C++, C and Fortran77(!).

Transferable Skills

I have an MSc in geophysics and enjoy working with data. More recently I have developed my skills in data analysis through curiosity driven projects. I experimented with a variety of data visualisations creating maps, barcharts, smoothed time series and statistical plots of aggregated data. These charts evolved from basic graphs using default options to more carefully designed layouts following data visualisation best practice for maximum insight.

I consider myself quite flexible and a team player, always willing to adapt as the need arises. I have supported a number of clubs through work on committees - e.g. health & safety rep at the pre-school, secretary for the running club. I have worked with both the general public stewarding art exhibitions and dealing with tenancy applications and the more technically minded. I love to learn and have made use of online courses, books, blogs and websites to pick up what I needed for the project in hand - whether it be a simple website for a local art group, or playing with the development of a connect4 game using reinforcement learning.

I spent a year tutoring maths at all levels up to A level. I also volunteered at a local college supporting adult learners with numeracy.

Why Tech Returners?

I was introduced to the Manchester Tech Returners Programme by a close friend. After reading their online blurb and case studies it seemed just what I needed...

I moved for the job many moons ago. As a result I had no family support to help juggle child care. I abandoned a career in tech due to the need for flexible employment - no need for violins. I have no regrets. But it has meant a lengthy break from directly relevant work experience and my current tech skills are largely self-taught.

I still love the satisfaction of working through a problem and looking at data. I would love to make it more of my day job. The tech returners programme will give me the opportunity to show what I can do and take me the next step on my journey.
